Call for participation

This workshop explores how MR is becoming embedded in everyday routines and asks how we can design seamless, inclusive, and meaningful transitions between the physical and the virtual.

Topics of interest

  • Seamless transitions: metaphors, interface concepts, and technical solutions across VR/MR/physical contexts
  • Onboarding & accessibility: inclusive interaction patterns across culture, disabilities, and differing tech experience
  • Embodied everyday interaction: cooking, commuting, healthcare, education, work
  • Speculative & critical perspectives: ethics, opting in/out, continuous recording and privacy
  • Prototypes & demonstrations: systems, mock-ups, artworks, and future scenarios

Submission types

  • Short papers: up to 6 pages
  • Position papers: 2–4 pages
  • Poster/demo abstracts: 1 page
Submission instructions
Submit via the Google Form: https://forms.gle/xUcm8cPiDVoDgMyN6.
Open submission form

Participation note: At least one author of each accepted submission must register and attend the workshop.

Publication & sharing

  • Accepted papers/posters/demos will be collected into proceedings (planned: CEUR-WS).
  • Materials (slides, photos of prototypes, summaries) will be hosted on this website after the workshop.
  • A mirror archive will be created on Zenodo to ensure long-term access and DOI-based citation.
  • Organizers plan a post-workshop report for broader dissemination (e.g., SIGCHI channels).
