Activity details

Introduction & scene setting

We start with goals, key research questions, and a shared framing of XR as everyday technology across the reality–virtuality continuum.

Paper presentations

Short presentations (5–7 minutes each) of accepted contributions to seed discussion, establish shared vocabulary, and surface tensions.

Debate & critical reflections

A moderated discussion on ethics and trade-offs: privacy, continuous recording, opting in/out, and how inclusiveness changes what “seamless” should mean.

Prototype demos & posters

Hands-on sharing of prototypes and demo concepts (e.g., carrying virtual objects across transitions, scalable overlays, assistive MR interaction patterns).

Breakout groups & co-design

Small groups co-design solutions and principles around everyday usability, onboarding, accessibility, and cultural/ethical considerations.

Sharing, synthesis & wrap-up

Groups report back; we synthesize design principles and open research challenges, and align on post-workshop outcomes and publishing.

Post-workshop (planned)

  • Proceedings (CEUR-WS) and a Zenodo mirror with DOI-based citation
  • Website update with accepted materials, photos, and a short synthesis report
