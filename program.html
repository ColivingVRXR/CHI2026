Program

CHI 2026 Workshop • Barcelona, Spain • April 13–17, 2026

Activity details

Welcome & opening provocation

The session opens with a live demonstration of the workshop's central theme: a co-organiser joins remotely as a virtual avatar rendered live in the physical space. Participants experience XR co-presence before a word of theory is spoken.

The Lightning Round

20 accepted papers presented in strict 3-minute slots, ordered by thematic group. One guiding question for every presenter: ”How does my work change the way a human experiences their physical home or self?” No related-work slides.

Provocation Round

Five deliberately divisive statements are read aloud; the room votes agree / disagree / unsure by show of hands with no debate. The goal is to leave participants divided and curious, seeding conversations that carry through the break.

Phase 1 — Expert Workstreams

Participants work in their 4 thematic groups to distill their collective research into exactly 2 Universal Guidelines — one technical, one ethical — using the Golden Rule Template.

Phase 2 — The 24-Hour Stress Test

A Jigsaw Swap recombines participants into 4 mixed groups. Each group receives a Persona Card and maps their persona's full day (08:00–22:00), applying at least 3 technologies and 4 Golden Rules. The critical question: where do the rules fail?

Phase 3 — Manifesto Reveal

Each mixed group presents their Conflict Point — the gap in current research that the field must solve next. The session closes with 8 Golden Rules and 4 Persona Maps on the wall: the Co-Living Design & Ethics Manifesto.

Post-workshop (planned)

  • Proceedings (CEUR-WS) and a Zenodo mirror with DOI-based citation
  • Website update with accepted materials, photos, and a short synthesis report
